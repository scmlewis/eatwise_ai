## Brand Overview

### Mission
EatWise empowers individuals to understand and optimize their nutrition through intelligent meal tracking and AI-powered insights.

### Vision
Make healthy eating accessible, intuitive, and scientifically-backed for everyone.

### Brand Values
- **Intelligence** - AI-driven insights, data-focused
- **Wellness** - Health-first approach, positive psychology
- **Simplicity** - Intuitive interfaces, no jargon
- **Trust** - Accurate data, transparent science
- **Empowerment** - User control, personalization
- **Community** - Shared goals, mutual support

### Target Audience
- Health-conscious individuals (18-50 years old)
- Fitness enthusiasts and athletes
- People managing health conditions through nutrition
- Users seeking positive lifestyle change
- Tech-savvy wellness seekers

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice Characteristics
- **Friendly** - Approachable, not clinical
- **Motivating** - Encouraging, positive reinforcement
- **Clear** - Simple, jargon-free language
- **Intelligent** - Evidence-based, science-backed
- **Empowering** - User-focused, respectful

### Tone Variations by Context

#### Achievement/Positive Feedback
*Tone: Celebratory, enthusiastic*
- "üî• Amazing! You've hit a 7-day streak!"
- "üéâ Daily goal achieved! Great work!"
- "‚úÖ You're on track to your goals!"

#### Motivation/Gentle Nudges
*Tone: Encouraging, supportive*
- "üí™ Great progress! Let's keep the momentum going."
- "üìù Don't forget to log your meals today!"
- "üí° Here's a tip for better nutrition..."

#### Alerts/Cautions
*Tone: Informative, helpful, not judgmental*
- "‚ö†Ô∏è This meal exceeds your sodium target"
- "üìä Your sugar intake is higher than usual"
- "üí° Consider: swapping this with a healthier option"

#### Instructions/Help
*Tone: Clear, step-by-step, supportive*
- "Take a photo of your meal or describe it in text"
- "Select the meal type from the options below"
- "Your nutrition analysis will appear in seconds"

### Language Guidelines

**Do:**
- ‚úÖ Use "you" and "your" (personal, engaging)
- ‚úÖ Use positive framing ("Increase protein" vs "Decrease carbs")
- ‚úÖ Celebrate small wins and progress
- ‚úÖ Explain the "why" behind recommendations
- ‚úÖ Use emojis appropriately (one per message max)
- ‚úÖ Break complex info into digestible chunks

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Use medical/clinical jargon without explanation
- ‚ùå Be judgmental or shame-based ("You ate badly")
- ‚ùå Make promises you can't keep
- ‚ùå Use excessive exclamation marks (!!!)
- ‚ùå Overcomplicate simple messages
- ‚ùå Ignore accessibility (describe emojis)

## Visual Style

### Design Principles

1. **Minimalist** - Clean interfaces, essential elements only
2. **Geometric** - Use of shapes, grids, and structured layouts
3. **Modern** - Contemporary aesthetics, smooth transitions
4. **Accessible** - High contrast, readable fonts, clear hierarchy
5. **Consistent** - Uniform spacing, sizing, and styling

### Component Style

#### Cards & Containers
- **Corners:** 12px border radius minimum
- **Shadows:** Subtle, 0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.1)
- **Background:** Transparent with 5-10% color overlay
- **Border:** 1-2px solid with color palette colors

#### Buttons
- **Style:** Gradient background, rounded corners (12px)
- **States:**
  - **Default:** Primary gradient, full opacity
  - **Hover:** Slight elevation, increased shadow
  - **Active:** Darker gradient, scale 0.98
  - **Disabled:** 60% opacity, no cursor change

#### Icons
- **Size Ranges:** 16px, 24px, 32px, 48px, 64px
- **Stroke Width:** 2-3px for outlined icons
- **Style:** Simple, geometric, consistent weight

#### Progress Bars
- **Background:** Subtle color, 10% opacity
- **Fill:** Color gradient (Primary ‚Üí Light Teal)
- **Height:** 6-8px for thin bars, 12-16px for prominent
- **Animation:** Smooth 0.3s transition

### Animation & Motion

**Principles:**
- Purpose-driven (not decorative)
- Quick (200-400ms for UI elements)
- Smooth (cubic-bezier easing)
- Accessible (respects prefers-reduced-motion)

**Common Transitions:**
- **Hover States:** 200ms ease
- **Page Transitions:** 300ms fade-in
- **Loading:** 1.5s infinite shimmer
- **Success Feedback:** 400ms scale bounce

### Layout & Spacing

**Base Unit:** 8px  
**Spacing Scale:**
- Extra Small: 4px
- Small: 8px
- Medium: 16px
- Large: 24px
- Extra Large: 32px
- Section: 48px+

**Responsive Breakpoints:**
- Mobile: < 640px
- Tablet: 640px - 1024px
- Desktop: > 1024px

## Do's & Don'ts

### Logo Do's ‚úÖ
- ‚úÖ Maintain proper clear space (20px minimum)
- ‚úÖ Use provided SVG files (highest quality)
- ‚úÖ Scale proportionally
- ‚úÖ Ensure sufficient contrast against backgrounds
- ‚úÖ Use color variations for different backgrounds
- ‚úÖ Place on solid or subtle backgrounds
- ‚úÖ Test at multiple sizes before finalizing

### Logo Don'ts ‚ùå
- ‚ùå Don't skew, stretch, or distort
- ‚ùå Don't add effects (shadows, glows, 3D)
- ‚ùå Don't rotate at odd angles
- ‚ùå Don't use below 16px
- ‚ùå Don't add borders or outlines
- ‚ùå Don't combine with other logos
- ‚ùå Don't change colors arbitrarily
- ‚ùå Don't place on busy backgrounds

### Color Do's ‚úÖ
- ‚úÖ Use primary teal for main actions and trust elements
- ‚úÖ Use coral for attention-grabbing CTAs
- ‚úÖ Use green for positive feedback and achievements
- ‚úÖ Use yellow for cautions and warnings
- ‚úÖ Maintain sufficient contrast for accessibility
- ‚úÖ Test colors on actual backgrounds
- ‚úÖ Use gradients for visual interest (primary to light teal)

### Color Don'ts ‚ùå
- ‚ùå Don't mix too many colors (stick to palette)
- ‚ùå Don't use colors only for meaning (add icons/labels)
- ‚ùå Don't reduce contrast below WCAG AA standards
- ‚ùå Don't use coral for large text areas (hard on eyes)
- ‚ùå Don't invent new colors that "match"
- ‚ùå Don't use full saturation yellow for backgrounds
- ‚ùå Don't forget about color blindness

### Typography Do's ‚úÖ
- ‚úÖ Use Outfit for all brand materials
- ‚úÖ Match the tone to the context
- ‚úÖ Use clear hierarchy (sizes, weights)
- ‚úÖ Break up long text into chunks
- ‚úÖ Use meaningful emoji (max 1 per message)
- ‚úÖ Be consistent with formatting
- ‚úÖ Proofread for accuracy

### Typography Don'ts ‚ùå
- ‚ùå Don't mix multiple font families
- ‚ùå Don't use ALL CAPS for body text
- ‚ùå Don't make fonts smaller than 12px
- ‚ùå Don't use italic for body text
- ‚ùå Don't forget line height (minimum 1.4)
- ‚ùå Don't use jargon without explanation
- ‚ùå Don't overuse exclamation marks

### Voice & Tone Do's ‚úÖ
- ‚úÖ Be friendly and approachable
- ‚úÖ Celebrate user achievements
- ‚úÖ Explain the reasoning
- ‚úÖ Use "you" and "your"
- ‚úÖ Provide actionable suggestions
- ‚úÖ Be positive and motivating
- ‚úÖ Respect user intelligence

### Voice & Tone Don'ts ‚ùå
- ‚ùå Don't be clinical or robotic
- ‚ùå Don't shame or judge users
- ‚ùå Don't use complex jargon
- ‚ùå Don't make false promises
- ‚ùå Don't be overly casual
- ‚ùå Don't ignore edge cases
- ‚ùå Don't overwhelm with information
